Aeration







Key Points to Aeration




  • Mitigates problems with offensive odors
  • Degrades vaccines, antibiotics, deworming products and pesticides
  • Controls the pile temperature, destroying pathogens, parasites and weed seeds
  • Significantly increases the rate of composting
  • Converts a raw waste into a humus-rich soil
  • It is ideal for pre-composting feedstocks for vermi-composting
  • Produces amazing quality compost
  • Produces a compost rich in aerobic microbes







    • Oxygen Level




  • An aerated pile of composting should be over 550C (1310F) for a minimum of 3-4
    days to ensure that there is pathogen destruction and to produce a safe product.
  • When air is first introduced into a (non-aerated) compost pile, the
    microorganisms that are already in the mix go crazy and go over 1500F
    in less than 24 hours. I should take advantage of this heat and make it
    destroy pathogens, parasites and weed seeds.
  • With aeration, the increased number and diversity of microorganisms greatly quicken the
    composting process. With aerated composting, the active phase typically

    lasts 21 to 30 days and the subsequent curing phase lasts an additional 30 to 60 days.
  • The goal is to maintain the oxygen level at 8% or higher. The main by-products are water,
    heat, carbon and dioxide. With aerobic composting, the quality of the odor is often described as peasant.
